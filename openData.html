San Jose improves traffic safety through data


In 2015, San Jose experienced the highest number of traffic fatalities in the last 20 years. By leveraging data, the city accident hotspots to start bringing those down to zero


Behavioral science helps keep San Jose clean


San Jose’s data team and Environmental Service Department worked with Bloomberg Foundation’s “What Works Cities” to apply cutting-edge behavioral science techniques to increase the use of free large bulky item collection services


Data shows the city working for you


From being able to better target code enforcement, to helping homes with the highest fire risk, to identifying “hot spots” for crime, and much more, data helps us foster a performance-driven transparent government focused on constant improvement




CALL TO ACTION:

You are the most important part of our city, and, we hope you can leverage our data to make government more transparent, efficient and smarter. Make dashboards, data visualizations, tools for transparency, and more, to help reinvent our local democracy and re-define what it means to be a citizen in the Capital of Silicon Valley.
