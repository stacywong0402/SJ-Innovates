Khanh Duy Russo

Director of Strategic Partnerships, and LGBT Liaison


Khanh Duy Russo is a Sr. Policy Advisor, Director of Strategic Partnership, and the Mayor's LGBT Liaison to the Community. In that role, he develops a shared agenda and tangible strategies with partners that leverages innovation, community resources and investments for creative public problem solving. Prior to joining the Mayor's office, he worked at Cisco where he guided the global strategy and implementation of internet and technology based solutions addressing critical human needs. This included strategies to alleviate hunger and homelessness, increase access to water and disaster relief. He has also held positions at Silicon Valley Community Foundation, Kaiser Permanente, North Hills Community Outreach, and The Forbes Funds. He holds a Master of Science in Public Policy and Management from Carnegie Mellon University, a Bachelor’s in Political Science and Religious Studies from Santa Clara University, and was a Coro Northern California Public Affairs Fellow. Khanh speaks fluent Vietnamese and is conversant in Spanish. He and his husband currently reside in Downtown San José.

Shireen Santosham

Chief Innovation Officer


Shireen Santosham is Chief Innovation Officer for Mayor Liccardo where she leads efforts to build the "smart city" vision and strategy for San Jose. Previously, she worked at the GSMA where she was a Director in the Mobile for Development group that aims to close the digital divide for base of the pyramid populations in the developing world through mobile technology and the internet. She has extensive experience working on innovative initiatives across sectors, including working at McKinsey & Company, with international NGOs, and as an impact investor at Vulcan Capital. Shireen holds a M.B.A. from Harvard Business School and an M.P.A in International Development from Harvard Kennedy School and is a term member at the Council on Foreign Relations.

Ingrid Holguin

Policy Advisor


Ingrid Holguin joined the Office of the Mayor of San Jose, Sam Liccardo as Policy Advisor for Strategic Partnerships in early 2015. Ms. Holguin has over 20 years of experience working on issues of economic and community development with a passion for building financial capabilities for individuals and institutions. Most recently she was Director of Savings Programs for Opportunity Fund, a San Jose-headquartered Community Development Financial Institution. As a consultant for over ten years and prior work as Program Officer for the Ford Foundation in Mexico City, working in Mexico and Central America, her work has included philanthropy, impact investing, strategic planning, evaluation andprogram design. Ms. Holguin holds an MBA from the University of California – Berkeley, Walter A. Haas School of Business and a B.A. in Economics and Management from Ball State University. She speaks fluent Spanish, lives in San Jose with her husband and teenage daughter and is proud mother of an AmeriCorps VISTA Teaching Fellow at an East San Jose middle school extended learning program.
