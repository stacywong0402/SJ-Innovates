Partner With Us

Metro Lab

In June 2015, San José joined a university-city collaborative called “MetroLab” with San Jose State University and Carnegie Mellon University.

This partnership helps us research and deploy projects that meaningfully improve the city’s transportation infrastructure, water and sewer systems, communication mediums, and public service delivery models.

These partnerships benefit the university, by providing the opportunity to conduct research in a living urban laboratory; and the city can then leverage these ideas into policy decisions to improve the lives of our residents.

What Works Cities

San José has partnered with Bloomberg Philanthropies’ “What Works Cities,” a nonprofit that provides financial support for cities to expand the use of data and evidence in their policymaking.

In December 2015, Bloomberg chose San Jose as one of 13 cities to receive funding. San Jose has used this grant to establish and improve open data practices, develop the capacity to conduct real-time evaluations of service delivery, and create a data-minded culture throughout the City.

Through this partnership, San José has sought to establish a system within City Hall that outlasts political change, fuels culture change, and creates an enduring capacity for greater innovation. Bloomberg Philanthropies has helped the mayor’s office and city hall develop an internal ecosystem to execute on this vision. The group helped support the launch of the Office of Civic Innovation in the City Manager’s Office, hiring the first-ever Chief Innovation Officer in San Jose, and hiring a Deputy City Attorney focused on innovation.
