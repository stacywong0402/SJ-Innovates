ProspectSV

Your Launchpad for Innovation


San Jose has paved the way for tomorrow’s transportation by creating a space for innovation, demonstration and partnership. San Jose’s story started nearly a decade ago when the city became created a “Technology Demonstration Partnership Policy” to give companies access to its facilities for pre-commercial technology demonstration.

 

Quickly, innovators’ demand for the space in became clear: this policy led to the demonstration of 16 new clean tech projects, including the first-ever solar-powered electric vehicle charging stations.

 

We built on this model with an approach focused on transportation.

 

Silicon Valley innovators asked for the ability to develop transportation technologies in a real-world setting. In response, we founded our non-profit partner, ProspectSV, and launched a “Technology Demonstration Center” in 2014.

 

This $12 million, 23,000 square foot Technology Demonstration Center provides workspace and resources for local technology companies to innovate and demonstrate cutting-edge technology. The Center is home to two labs: The Intelligent Traffic Systems Lab (ITS Lab) provides hardware and software tools to test advanced technology related to concepts like traffic flow management. The SimLab provides a simulator with a real car and a 220-degree multi-screen surround view to create an immersive virtual environment to test cutting edge concepts in transportation that require compliance with National Highway Traffic Safety Administration guidelines.

 

These projects, and the many more tested in ProspectSV, allow for the technology reshaping our world to get its start in San Jose and demonstrate how we create outside structures to incubate innovative solutions.

 

Transportation Innovation Zone

Paving The Way for Tomorrow’s Transportation


In 2014, we created a “Transportation Innovation Zone” located in North San Jose that offers a multi-mile network of streets, signals, and infrastructure as a platform for technology innovators. With 21 traffic signals, over 670 streetlights and standard public transit infrastructure – this zone serves as the perfect playground to develop tomorrow’s self-driving vehicles, automated traffic enforcement systems, vehicle to infrastructure tools, streetlight innovations, traffic sensors and more.

 

The TIZ has attracted corporate partners across Silicon Valley: Cisco and partners are testing new Vehicle–Infrastructure communications technology employing Dedicated Short Range Communications (DSRC) and traffic analytics approaches; Phillips is installing Smart Poles, that allow for enhanced cellular communications, and employ PG&E wireless Smart Meter technology; BMW and Toyota use traffic signal data feeds to test smart vehicle applications; and leading technology companies are helping us build an autonomous vehicle zone to accelerate the next wave of evolution in how we get around. Innovation like this is moving us towards our goal of becoming the premier global hub of urban technology innovation for smart cities.
