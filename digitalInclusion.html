Facebook "Terragraph"

The City of San Jose will be the first city in the world to pioneer Facebook’s new wireless “Terragraph” technology. Terragraph serves as a cutting-edge new way to connect to the Internet— delivering high-speed Internet at one-gigabit rates wirelessly without installing fiber optic cable underground. Beta testing has begun and will continue until late 2017. If successful, Terragraph will help us bridge our digital divide by providing low-income communities with more affordable high-speed Internet options.

East Side Union High School District Wi-Fi

In September of 2016, the City of San Jose launched a pilot deployment of a free Wi-Fi network to serve East Side Union High School District families. This partnership between the City and District hopes to bridge the digital divide by connecting more underserved and low-income students with affordable broadband Internet service.

Speed Up San Jose

“SpeedUp San Jose” is an initiative that helps us determine the real Internet speed that our residents experience. This knowledge helps us fill gaps in service delivery to ensure San Jose stays well connected, but we can’t do this alone. We need your help to learn which neighborhoods consistently have slow Internet connections. Help us by taking a speed test here.
